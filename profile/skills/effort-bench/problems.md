# Effort Benchmark — Problems

Solve all 6 from scratch, in order. For each, show brief reasoning (2-4 lines)
and end with `ANSWER N: <value>`. Do NOT open answers.md until all 6 are done.

---

**Problem 1.** If the day before yesterday was a Thursday, what day of the week
will it be 100 days from today?

---

**Problem 2.** A factory has 5 machines that together produce 5 widgets in 5
minutes. Assuming machines work independently at the same rate, how long would
100 machines take to produce 100 widgets?

---

**Problem 3.** You roll two fair six-sided dice. Given that at least one die
shows a 6, what is the probability that *both* dice show 6? Give the answer as a
fraction in lowest terms.

---

**Problem 4.** On an island, knights always tell the truth and knaves always
lie. Every inhabitant is exactly one of the two. You meet three people, A, B,
and C.
- A says: "B and C are both knaves."
- B says: "A is a knight."

Classify each of A, B, and C as knight or knave.

---

**Problem 5.** How many positive integers from 1 to 1000 inclusive are divisible
by neither 3 nor 5?

---

**Problem 6.** You have a balance scale and may use it exactly 3 times. Among a
pile of identical-looking coins, exactly one is counterfeit; it weighs slightly
more or slightly less than a genuine coin, but you do not know which. You have no
known-good reference coin. What is the maximum number of coins for which you can
ALWAYS, in 3 weighings, both identify the counterfeit AND determine whether it is
heavier or lighter? Give a single integer.
